e-Portfolio Activity ‚Äì GDPR Case Study

Case Study: Disclosure of CCTV footage from a direct provision centre (2018). The case involved the alleged disclosure of personal data via CCTV footage at a State-run accommodation centre.

Specific GDPR Aspect Addressed

The Data Protection Commission (DPC) identified breaches of the Data Protection Acts 1988 and 2003:

  • Section 4: Failure to respond to an access request within 40 days.
  • Section 2(1)(d): Lack of adequate security measures to prevent unauthorised disclosure.
  • Section 2C(2) & 2C(3): No written agreement between the controller (RIA) and processor (Aramark) defining data-handling obligations.

Resolution

The DPC found that both the RIA and Aramark failed in their duty of care by not implementing sufficient security controls or clear procedures for data transmission. Although it could not be proven whether the footage reached the radio station, the absence of documented policies, contracts, and staff awareness led to infringements of the complainant‚Äôs data protection rights.

Steps as Information Security Manager

  • Enforce written Data Processing Agreements with all processors defining security obligations.
  • Implement strict access controls, encryption, and secure transfer methods for personal data.
  • Ensure GDPR access request timelines are always met.
  • Create clear policies for CCTV usage, data storage, and network transfers.
  • Conduct staff awareness training on GDPR compliance and incident reporting.
  • Perform regular audits to identify risks and gaps in data protection practices.
