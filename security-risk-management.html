Unit 1: An Introduction to Security and Risk Management

Lecturecast • 1 hr Reading • 7 hrs Formative activities

Focus

Definitions of risk and Security & Risk Management (SRM); threats vs vulnerabilities; the Risk Management Process (RMP); qualitative and quantitative assessment basics; common standards and when they apply.

Intended Learning

  • Discuss definitions of risk and SRM concepts.
  • Explain how to assess, qualify, mitigate risks; outline qualitative vs quantitative approaches.
  • List common security & risk standards and select appropriate ones for a scenario.
Discussion 1 (begins U1): Posted initial response on Industry 4.0, risk categories, and literature triangulation (Hancock et al., Kovaitė & Stankevičienė, +1 supporting/contrasting paper).

Unit 2: Users, Assessments and the Risk Management Process

Reading • 4 hrs Seminar activity Discussion peer responses

Focus

Deep dive into the RMP, the impact of assessment type (qualitative vs quantitative), and the role of user participation in risk identification, analysis, and treatment. Based on Spears & Barki (2010), plus seminar questions on AI-powered analytics for risk prediction and continuity.

Intended Learning

  • Differentiate qualitative vs quantitative assessments and their benefits/trade-offs.
  • Explain how user participation shapes RMP outcomes and mitigations.
  • Reflect on implications of recommended controls.
Seminar recording: Prepared answers on mixed-methods assessment, AI analytics integration beyond NLP, and multi-tech AI stacks in enterprise risk.

Unit 3: Introduction to Threat Modelling and Management

Lecturecast • 1 hr Reading • 5 hrs Discussion summary post

Focus

Threats vs vulnerabilities; survey of threat modelling techniques (STRIDE, DREAD, attack trees) and hybrid approaches like PASTA; when to combine frameworks and how cookbooks/guides inform selection.

Intended Learning

  • Describe multiple threat modelling techniques and selection criteria.
  • Advise technique choice for specific contexts; identify when to hybridise.
Discussion 1 (U3 wrap): Submitted a 300-word summary synthesising my initial post, peer feedback, and content across Units 1–3.

Unit 4: Application of Threat Modelling and Management Techniques

Reading • 8 hrs Formative activities • 6 hrs Seminar exercises

Focus

Practical threat modelling guided by the Threat Modelling Manifesto, OWASP cookbook, and MITRE ATT&CK. Built models with STRIDE/DREAD, attack trees, and referenced CVSS where relevant.

Workshop (Jbair et al., 2022)

  • Key CPS elements & interdependencies and why they matter for accurate analysis.
  • Entry points & vulnerabilities in cyber-physical energy systems; practical challenges.
  • Scenario-specific metrics for prioritisation; mapping to targeted countermeasures.
Prepared answers and discussed via seminar recording; captured notes and tables in artefacts.

Reflective Summary (Units 1–6)

Across Units 1–3 I moved from foundation concepts to method selection: distinguishing risk definitions, weighing qualitative vs quantitative assessments, and appreciating how user participation surfaces operational realities that impact the RMP. In Units 4–6, hands-on modelling (STRIDE/DREAD/attack trees/PASTA) and compliance framing (GDPR, PCI-DSS, ISO 27000) helped me connect technical threats to business standards and control requirements. Watching seminar recordings let me pause/rewind complex sections and translate them into actionable artefacts. Key improvements ahead: deepen CVSS scoring practice, prototype hybrid modelling templates for repeatable analysis, and maintain a live standards checklist tied to business context and data flows.
