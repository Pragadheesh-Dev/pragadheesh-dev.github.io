Module Overview

This ePortfolio documents my work for Security & Risk Management through Units 1–6: foundations of risk, the Risk Management Process (RMP), qualitative vs quantitative assessments, threat modelling methods (STRIDE, DREAD, attack trees, PASTA), and security standards (GDPR, PCI-DSS, ISO 27000). It also captures formative activities, seminar prep, team artefacts, and reflections.

Critical thinking Risk analysis Threat modelling Standards & compliance Teamwork Professional writing

Unit 1: An Introduction to Security and Risk Management

Lecturecast: 1 hr | Reading: 7 hrs | Formative activities

Definitions of risk, Security & Risk Management (SRM), threats vs vulnerabilities, Risk Management Process (RMP), qualitative vs quantitative approaches, risk standards selection.

Unit 2: Users, Assessments and the Risk Management Process

Reading: 4 hrs | Seminar Prep: AI in Risk Management Case Study

Deeper dive into RMP, effects of qualitative vs quantitative assessments, and the role of user participation in risk processes.

Unit 3: Introduction to Threat Modelling and Management

Lecturecast: 1 hr | Reading: 5 hrs | Discussion: Threat Modelling Approaches

Introduces STRIDE, DREAD, Attack Trees, PASTA, and hybrid approaches for risk and threat modelling.

Unit 4: Application of Threat Modelling and Management Techniques

Workshop-based application of STRIDE, DREAD, PASTA, MITRE ATT&CK for practical threat modelling scenarios.

Unit 5: An Introduction to Security and Risk Standards in Industry and the Enterprise

Review of security standards like GDPR, PCI-DSS, ISO 27000 family, and their role in enterprise risk management.

Unit 6: The Practical Implications of Security and Risk Standards

Applying standards to real-world case studies; GDPR compliance, PCI-DSS assessments, and mitigation planning.

Team & Artefacts

Reflection (Units 1–6)

Personal reflections on learning progress, key insights, and skill development across all units.
